# OSINT Summary

## Definition
OSINT = intelligence from collecting + analyzing publicly available info to answer specific question.

## Info ≠ Intelligence
Raw data becomes intelligence only after analysis + critical thinking.

## Sources
Public records, news, libraries, social media, images/videos, websites, dark web.

## Users
Gov, law enforcement, military, journalists, investigators, infosec, CTI, pen testers, social engineers.

## Intelligence Cycle
1. **Preparation** — define objectives, identify sources
2. **Collection** — gather from relevant sources
3. **Processing** — organize/filter data
4. **Analysis & Production** — interpret, find patterns, report
5. **Dissemination** — deliver findings to stakeholders

## Passive vs Active
- **Passive**: no target engagement (search only)
- **Active**: engage target (friend/like/message) — may require undercover protocols

## Ethics
Legal boundaries, avoid harm, respect privacy, integrity/accuracy, purpose limitation, transparency.

## OSINT4Good
- Trace Labs — Search Party CTF finds missing persons
- Europol — "Stop Child Abuse – Trace an Object" campaign

## Organizational Benefits
Criminal investigations, risk assessments, decision support, entity association, situational awareness.
